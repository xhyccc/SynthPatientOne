# Department Hierarchy Reference

Complete international standard hospital department classification used by the patient profile builder.

---

## 1. Internal Medicine

Focuses on the diagnosis and non-surgical treatment of adult diseases.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Cardiology | Heart and blood vessels | BP, HR, lipids, ECG, echo, troponin, BNP, family history of CAD |
| 1.2 | Pulmonology | Lungs and respiratory tract | Smoking history, SpO₂, PFTs, CXR, ABG, sputum culture |
| 1.3 | Gastroenterology | Digestive system and intestines | LFTs, endoscopy reports, H. pylori, stool studies, abdominal imaging |
| 1.4 | Endocrinology | Hormones and metabolic disorders | HbA1c, fasting glucose, TSH, T3/T4, cortisol, bone density |
| 1.5 | Nephrology | Kidneys | Serum Cr, BUN, eGFR, urine protein, electrolytes, renal US |
| 1.6 | Neurology | Brain, spinal cord, nervous system | Mental status, cranial nerves, motor/sensory exam, CT/MRI brain, EEG |
| 1.7 | Rheumatology | Joints, muscles, autoimmune | ESR, CRP, ANA, RF, anti-CCP, joint exam, imaging |
| 1.8 | Hematology | Blood and blood-producing organs | CBC, ferritin, B12, folate, PT/PTT, peripheral smear |
| 1.9 | Oncology | Cancer diagnosis and treatment | Tumor markers, biopsy results, staging scans, treatment history |
| 1.10 | Infectious Diseases | Contagious and communicable diseases | WBC, CRP, procalcitonin, cultures, serologies, travel history |

## 2. Surgery

Manages operative procedures.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | General Surgery | Abdominal organs | Surgical history, abdominal exam, CT abdomen, pre-op labs |
| 2.2 | Orthopedic Surgery | Bones, joints, ligaments, tendons | X-rays, MRI joints, DEXA, surgical history, range of motion |
| 2.3 | Neurosurgery | Brain and nervous system (operative) | CT/MRI brain/spine, neuro exam, ICP monitoring |
| 2.4 | Cardiothoracic Surgery | Heart, lungs, chest cavity | Echo, cardiac catheterization, CXR, PFTs, surgical history |
| 2.5 | Urology | Urinary tract, male reproductive | UA, urine culture, PSA, creatinine, cystoscopy reports |
| 2.6 | Plastic & Reconstructive | Restoration/reconstruction | Wound assessment, surgical history, photographs, skin assessment |
| 2.7 | Vascular Surgery | Arteries and veins | ABI, vascular US, CT/MR angiography, smoking history, wound exam |

## 3. Obstetrics and Gynecology (OB/GYN)

Focuses on the female reproductive system and childbirth.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Obstetrics | Pregnancy, childbirth, postpartum | Gravida/para, LMP, EDD, prenatal labs, US reports, delivery history |
| 3.2 | Gynecology | Female reproductive health | Pap smear, HPV status, menstrual history, pelvic exam, STI screening |
| 3.3 | Reproductive Endocrinology | Infertility, hormonal issues | FSH, LH, estradiol, AMH, semen analysis, ovulation tracking |
| 3.4 | Gynecologic Oncology | Reproductive system cancers | CA-125, biopsy results, staging, surgical history, BRCA status |

## 4. Pediatrics

Medical care of infants, children, and adolescents.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | General Pediatrics | Routine care, common childhood illnesses | Growth charts, vaccination record, developmental milestones |
| 4.2 | Neonatology | Newborn infants (ill/premature) | Birth weight, Apgar scores, gestational age, NICU course |
| 4.3 | Pediatric Sub-specialties | Cardiology/Oncology/Neurology for children | Mirror adult variables + age-adjusted norms |

## 5. Emergency Medicine

Urgent and life-threatening conditions.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Trauma | Severe physical injuries | Mechanism of injury, GCS, trauma series imaging, FAST exam |
| 5.2 | Toxicology | Poisoning, drug overdoses | Tox screen, acetaminophen/salicylate levels, ECG, anion gap |
| 5.3 | Pre-hospital Care / EMS | Paramedic and ambulance services | Initial vitals, GCS at scene, interventions en route, time of onset |

## 6. Specialized Clinical Departments

Stand-alone first-tier departments.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6.1 | Psychiatry | Mental health, mood, psychotic disorders | MSE, PHQ-9, GAD-7, psychiatric history, medications, suicide risk |
| 6.2 | Ophthalmology | Eye and vision care | Visual acuity, IOP, fundoscopic exam, slit lamp exam |
| 6.3 | Otolaryngology (ENT) | Ear, nose, throat | Hearing test, nasal exam, throat exam, audiometry |
| 6.4 | Dermatology | Skin, hair, nails | Skin exam, lesion description, biopsy results, allergy history |

## 7. Medical Technology & Ancillary Departments

Support clinical teams in diagnosing and treating patients.

| # | Secondary Department | Focus Area | Key Expected Variables |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7.1 | Radiology / Imaging | X-rays, MRIs, CTs, ultrasounds | All imaging reports, contrast allergies, prior imaging comparisons |
| 7.2 | Pathology | Tissues and cells examination | Biopsy results, cytology, autopsy reports, special stains |
| 7.3 | Laboratory Medicine | Blood work, urinalysis, microbiology | All lab results with reference ranges, collection dates/times |
| 7.4 | Anesthesiology | Pain relief, vital function maintenance | Anesthesia history, airway assessment, allergies, prior complications |
| 7.5 | Rehabilitation / Physical Medicine | PT, OT, speech therapy | Functional assessments, therapy notes, mobility scores, ADL status |
